## Section 1 — Homepage commercial rewrite (Phase A)

### Why this comes first

The current homepage reads beautifully but reads to **no one specific**. "Adults navigating internal transitions" is everyone and no one. A woman 35–55 who Googles *"app for women feeling stuck"* lands, reads "internally disoriented, externally functioning," and doesn't recognise herself fast enough.

Commercial pages have a 5-second test: in 5 seconds, can the visitor answer *"is this for me?"* and *"what is it?"* If yes, they scroll. If no, they bounce.

The rewrite preserves the poetic voice. It adds a single specific naming layer above it: who this is for, what this is, how to try it free.

### 1.1 Hero — restructure

**Current state:** kicker "A turning point. A method." + title "A way back to yourself." + 3 body paragraphs + "Begin" CTA → /screening.

**Proposed structure:**

| Slot | Purpose | Draft (illustrative, you'll revise) |
|---|---|---|
| Kicker (small caps) | Audience naming | *"For women navigating midlife transitions"* |
| H1 (large serif) | Identity-naming + benefit | *"Quiet support for the women therapy missed."* — or — *"A self-help wellbeing companion for women in midlife who feel stuck."* |
| Sub-body (3-line max) | What this is in plain words | *"MindReset is a structured digital reflection platform. Not therapy, not coaching — a daily companion and an eight-stage method, built for women who already tried the obvious things."* |
| Primary CTA | Lowest-friction try | *"Try MiniMind free — 50 messages, no card"* → `/minimind` |
| Secondary CTA | For ready-to-commit visitors | *"Start the 5-minute check-in"* → `/screening` |
| Poetic line (small, beneath) | Keep brand voice | *"A way back to yourself."* (the original H1, demoted to subtitle) |

**Why this structure:** explicit audience naming above the fold + immediate free-try option + the poetic line preserved (just demoted from H1 to a smaller, intentional moment).

**Keywords this section must contain naturally:** "women in midlife", "self-help wellbeing", "daily companion", "structured" / "method", "stuck", "free 50 messages".

### 1.2 Who it is for — rewrite

**Current state:** 5 scenarios in literary English: "Feeling lost or disconnected despite doing everything right", "Emotional burnout after years of responsibility or caregiving"…

**Proposed structure:** keep the format but rewrite in the audience's own searchable language. The audience Googles in symptoms, not metaphors.

| Section header | *"This is for the women who…"* |
|---|---|
| Scenario 1 | "have read every book, journaled, tried therapy — and still feel something is off" |
| Scenario 2 | "wake up tired no matter how much they sleep" |
| Scenario 3 | "hold everything together for everyone, and don't know what they want anymore" |
| Scenario 4 | "can feel themselves disappearing under roles, expectations, people-pleasing" |
| Scenario 5 | "are not in crisis — just slowly, quietly losing themselves" |
| Closing line | *"It isn't therapy. It isn't a crisis service. It's where you go before either of those becomes necessary — or alongside, when one isn't enough."* |

**Keywords:** "people-pleasing", "exhaustion that sleep doesn't fix" *(or "tired no matter how much they sleep")*, "midlife", "self-abandonment" *(use the word in body copy)*, "feel stuck".

### 1.3 Method — describe without naming the 8 stages

**Why:** The Journey's 8-stage method is **IP**. Don't list the stages — that's giveaway. But the architecture must be **visible**, otherwise prospects can't trust there's real depth.

**Proposed structure:** a short, structured-prose section. Three paragraphs.

| Section header | *"The method, briefly"* (or *"What happens inside"*) |
|---|---|
| Paragraph 1 — what it is | *"The Journey is a structured inner reset built over many weeks. Not a meditation app. Not a chat bot. A sequenced path you walk at your own pace, designed by a founder who lived through the work herself."* |
| Paragraph 2 — what it does | *"We start by helping you find ground. Then we look at what's hurting — gently, without forcing it. We meet the parts of you that have been waiting to be heard. We separate what's actually yours from what was placed in you. We build a clearer sense of who you are now."* |
| Paragraph 3 — what it isn't | *"This isn't talk therapy. There's no diagnosis, no labels, no pressure. The method does the holding so you can do the work."* |

**Why this works:** describes the arc (stabilisation → pain identification → parts work → foreign material → integration) *without* using the internal stage names. AI search engines can quote it as a "what is the method" answer. Competitors can't reverse-engineer.

**Keywords:** "structured inner reset", "method", "self-paced", "without forcing it", "not therapy".

### 1.4 Pricing tier anchors — subtle, earned

**Why:** Visitors comparing options need anchor points. *"£14.99/month"* in isolation is meaningless. Anchored, it lands.

**Proposed treatment:** add a single line of context per tier on the homepage paths section (or pricing summary). Not a competitor takedown — just orientation.

| Tier | Current copy | Add a single line of context |
|---|---|---|
| MiniMind £14.99/mo | "A warm chat companion you can return to anytime…" | *"About the price of a Pret breakfast a week. Daily. Yours."* |
| States & Themes £29–£59 | "Short structured journeys for specific moments…" | *"Focused work on one thing at a time. Buy only what's heavy right now."* |
| The Journey £55/wk or £599 once | "A slow, sequential journey from stabilisation to a new sense of who you are…" | *"About what 6 hours of private therapy would cost. Months of structured work."* |

**Keywords:** none specifically — these are positioning, not keyword targets. They build value perception.

**Caveat:** the "6 hours of private therapy" anchor must not be heard as *"a substitute for therapy"*. The phrasing **"about what X would cost — months of work"** keeps it as a value frame, not a comparison of equivalence.

### 3.1 URL pattern recommendation

**Recommendation: `/journal`**

Reasoning:
- **Brand voice fit**: "journal" matches the reflective, contemplative tone (MindReset is about journaling-as-method, not "blog" content marketing)
- **SEO-neutral**: Google ranks `/journal`, `/blog`, `/articles`, `/insights` equally as long as content is good
- **Avoids "blog" connotations**: "blog" reads as casual / promotional; "journal" reads as thoughtful / authored
- **Indexable + memorable**: `mindreset.ai/journal/how-to-stop-people-pleasing-midlife` is clean and shareable

**Alternatives if you push back:**
- `/insights` — more authoritative, slightly more clinical-sounding
- `/articles` — neutral, professional, less voice-distinctive
- `/blog` — most SEO-conventional, but conflicts with brand voice

**Article URL pattern:** `/journal/{slug}` where slug is hyphen-separated, lowercase, ≤ 60 chars. Example: `/journal/how-to-stop-people-pleasing-in-midlife`.

### 3.3 Article structure template

**Length:** 1,200–2,000 words. Long enough to rank, short enough to read.

**Structure:**

```
H1: <evocative claim or direct question — matches search query>
   [intro paragraph — 3–5 lines, plain prose, answers the query
    in the first 100 words. AI extracts these.]

H2: <first sub-question>
   [body — 2–4 paragraphs]
   [optional pull-quote]

H2: <second sub-question>
   [body]
   [optional: small reflection prompt — 1–2 sentences,
    "Try this: notice what comes up when you read…"]

H2: <third sub-question>
   [body]

H2: One small thing you can do today
   [single concrete practice — short, doable, written in
    method voice]

H2: How MindReset works with this
   [3–5 lines that name the relevant product naturally,
    not as a hard sell]

H2: Frequently asked (3–5 short Q&As — FAQ schema territory)
   Q: …
   A: …

Footer:
   [signature line — "Written by Julia, founder of MindReset"]
   [1 line about MiniMind taster + CTA link]
```

**Structured data per article:**
- `Article` schema (author, datePublished, dateModified, image)
- `Person` for the author (Julia) — builds Person entity for AI search
- `FAQPage` schema for the embedded Q&A section
- Breadcrumb schema: Home → Journal → Article

**Internal linking per article (minimum):**
- 1 link to the relevant product page (MiniMind / States / Journey)
- 1 link to the FAQ
- 1 link to a related Journal article (once 2+ articles exist)
- 1 link to the homepage

**Image strategy:** photographic, atmospheric, one per article. No stock photos that look stock. Either real photography commissioned/sourced from Unsplash with intent, or pure text-only with elegant typography (matches brand voice). **Avoid:** AI-generated illustrative images that read as generic wellbeing-app stock.

### 3.4 Publishing cadence

**Recommendation: 2 articles per month for the first 6 months.**

Reasoning:
- Founder-written with Claude Code drafting can sustain 2/month realistically. 1/week burns out without dedicated content staff.
- 12 articles in 6 months gives Google enough surface area to assess domain expertise.
- After 6 months, drop to 1/month and double down on top performers (refresh, expand, link).

**Per-article workflow:**
- Claude Code drafts (1–2 hours, working from outline + Julia's notes)
- Julia revises in her voice (founder voice is critical — readers can tell)
- Claude Code wires up the page, schema, internal links (30 min)
- Add to sitemap, announce in newsletter, share on social

### 3.5 AI search optimisation per article

What makes an article citable by ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity:

1. **Direct factual answer in first 100 words.** AI extracts opening paragraphs.
2. **Clear Q&A structure** in body (matches how AI parses content).
3. **Article + FAQPage schema** — gives the model structured signal.
4. **Author byline with Person schema** — builds the entity ("Julia, founder of MindReset").
5. **Internal links to canonical product pages** — when AI cites the article, it has clear links to where to send the reader.
6. **No paywalls or auth gates** — AI crawlers can't see content behind logins.
7. **Updated `dateModified`** when refreshed — signals freshness.
8. **`llms-full.txt` references the article slugs** — makes them discoverable to AI crawlers that respect the spec.

## Section 4 — Comparison pages (Phase D)

Comparison pages capture **commercial-intent traffic** — people actively comparing products. Conversion rates are 3–10× content articles. Worth doing once domain authority is established.

### 4.1 URL pattern recommendation

**Recommendation: `/vs/{competitor}`**

Examples:
- `/vs/headspace`
- `/vs/wysa`
- `/vs/betterhelp`
- `/vs/calm`

Plus a plural alternatives page: `/alternatives` (lists the field, ranks for "MindReset alternatives" and "self-help wellbeing app alternatives").

**Why `/vs/`:** clean, short, clearly comparison-focused. SEO best practice 2026. Easy to expand to more competitors over time.

### 4.3 When to publish

**Not yet.** Comparison pages need domain authority to rank against Headspace's own SEO. Publishing them on a 0-authority domain wastes the SEO juice.

**Trigger:** publish the comparison pages **after 3–5 Journal articles are live and indexed** (estimated week 8–10 of the rollout). By then Google has signal that MindReset.ai is a real entity with topical depth, and the comparison pages will rank instead of getting buried.

**Order of comparison-page publication** (high to low priority):
1. `/vs/headspace` — biggest competitor search volume
2. `/alternatives` — captures comparison-shopper intent
3. `/vs/wysa` — direct AI-companion competitor, high relevance
4. `/vs/betterhelp` — captures therapy-considering intent
5. `/vs/calm` — meditation alternative, lower priority

### 5.4 Backlink strategy — first 6 months (realistic, ethical, founder-led)

**Goal:** 20–40 quality backlinks in 6 months. Not 200 spammy ones.

**What works for a small founder-led product:**

| Tactic | Effort | Realistic yield |
|---|---|---|

**Don't do:** paid link buying, PBN (private blog networks), comment spam, mass directory submission, link exchange schemes, AI-generated comment posting.
